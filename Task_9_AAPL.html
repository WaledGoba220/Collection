Dataset Summary

  • The dataset contains End of Day Daily Timeframe historical stock price data for Apple Inc. (AAPL) spanning from December 12, 1980, to September 09, 2025. It includes daily session open, high, low, close prices, and trading volume along with technical indicators such as the Stochastic Oscillator and volume moving average.
  • The original dataset shape is 11278 rows and 8 columns.
  • The data is downloaded from TradingView, a popular platform for financial market analysis and charting and is provided in CSV format for easy integration into data analysis and time series forecasting.

Notebook Summary

  • This notebook performs a basic Technical Analysis focusing on price action relative to the maximum and minimum values over specified periods that resemble support and resistance levels. It also includes the calculation different indicators and visualization of candlestick charts as well as volume and the Stochastic Oscillator which is a momentum indicator that compares a particular closing price of a security to a range of its prices over a certain period of time. The notebook uses Plotly for professional interactive visualizations that mimics TradingView charting layout, allowing users to explore the stock's price movements and technical indicators dynamically.

Features Documentation

  • time: datetime type that represents date in the format YYYY-MM-DD
  • open: The first opening price of the stock for the trading day.
  • high: The highest price reached by the stock during the trading day.
  • low: The lowest price reached by the stock during the trading day.
  • close: The last closing price of the stock for the trading day.
  • volume: The total number of shares traded during the trading day.
  • volume_ma: The moving average of the trading volume, which smooths out short-term fluctuations and highlights longer-term trends in volume.
  • p_k: The %K value of the Stochastic Oscillator, which indicates the current closing price relative to the high-low range over a specified period. This is a momentum indicator used to identify overbought or oversold conditions and the strength of the current price movement.

Short-Term Doncian Channel bands

doncian_upper, doncian_lower

  • These are simply the highest and lowest ranges for prices within a 20 bars period or almost a month when weekends are included.
  • I used shift(1) to show the penetration of important price levels also known as breakouts in technical analysis terms.
In[6]:
df['donchian_upper'] = df['high'].rolling(window=20).max().shift(1)
df['donchian_lower'] = df['low'].rolling(window=20).min().shift(1)

Long-Term Doncian Chennel Bands

donchian_upper_year, donchian_lower_year

  • These follow the same calculations but they have a span of 250 bars period which is almost a year including weekends and holidays.
In[7]:
df['donchian_upper_year'] = df['high'].rolling(window=250).max().shift(1)
df['donchian_lower_year'] = df['low'].rolling(window=250).min().shift(1)
